Anupama Mohan

Literature | Critical Theory | Ocean Studies | Environmental Humanities

Anupama Mohan is Associate Professor of English at the School of Liberal Arts in Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur. She is the author of the monograph Utopia and the Village in South Asian Literatures (Palgrave Macmillan UK, 2012) and the editor of two collections of essays on the theme of Transmodernity: "Maritime Transmodernities: Literatures of the Indian Ocean" (Special Issue, Postcolonial Text, 2019) and the two-part Special Issue Transmodernity and Literature: Of Worlds in Contact (Brill, July 2025 and Jan 2026). Her critical essays have featured in international peer-reviewed journals such as South Asian History and Culture, Asian Review of World Histories, University of Toronto Quarterly, Indian Theatre Journal, and Economic and Political Weekly, among others.

Anupama is also the writer of the novel Where Mayflies Live Forever (Picador, 2022) and the poetry collection, Twenty Odd Love Poems (The Writer's Workshop, 2008). She was the 2021 Writer-in-Residence at the Samyukta Research Foundation, Thiruvananthapuram and the 2024 Landhaus Fellow at the Rachel Carson Center for Environment and Society, University of Munich, Germany.

She is the Editor of Samyukta Fiction, the online digest of short stories housed in Samyukta Research Foundation.
